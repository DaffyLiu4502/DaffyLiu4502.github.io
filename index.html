Neighborhoods

Columbia Public Schools can only be as diverse as its neighborhoods

 Racial and socioeconomic segregation happens in school districts across the country; Redistricting of school attendance zones is worsening segregation in classrooms.  Redistricting of school boundaries is perpetuating racial segregation in neighborhoods and Columbia Public schools. The school district has opportunities to address segregation within the school district when they redraw school attendance boundaries. This then leads to a lack of resources for schools with a higher percentage of students of color. On a national level this is played out between school districts with the occurrence of white flight.
